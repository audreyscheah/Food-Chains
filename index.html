Introduction: Welcome to the Atlantic Ocean

As the planet's second largest body of water, the Atlantic Ocean hosts a vast variety of aquatic species. Biodiversity throughout its marine ecosystems includes (and exeeds) more than a thousand fish species along with numerous sharks, whales, and turtles.

Ecosystems and Food Chains Underwater

Underwater ecosystems, such as those in the Atlantic Ocean, rely on the interconnection of species within the food chain. Phytoplankton and zooplankton, the foundation of these ecosystems, provide energy and sustenance for a wide range of species, including small fish, jellyfish, and larger predators like sharks and whales.

The Effects of Climate Change

Climate change is disrupting ocean ecosystems by altering sea temperatures, acidifying waters, and affecting migration patterns. These changes can disrupt food availability for marine species and lead to a significantly negative impact on the entire food chain, from phytoplankton to top predators like sharks and whales.
